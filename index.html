Residnt Evil, known as Biohazard (バ イ オ ハ ザ ー ド) in Japan, is a series of video games, comics, novels, movies, and collectibles such as action figures, strategy guides, and other publications. The series was developed by the Capcom company and created by Shinji Mikami, the North American film series was created by Paul S. Anderson and the books by the writer S. D. Perry. Resident Evil could be translated into Spanish as "the evil that resides", while Biohazard is the term in English whose equivalent in Spanish would be "Biological Hazard".



The Umbrella Corporation is a multinational company, which is based in the United States, but has achieved great growth worldwide. Originally engaged in drug production, along with informatics and health systems and genetic engineering,

The T virus was used in a bioterrorist attack at Harvardville Airport, causing a massive infection of the entire terminal. Umbrella's other monstrosity, the G virus, was also used in the same outbreak.

Tyrant Virus, also known as t-Virus, is the general name given to a series of mutant strains of the Progenitor virus. Initially developed by Umbrella Pharmaceuticals in the late 1960s, the main goal of the "Project t-Virus" was two things: the virus had to be highly contagious to the point of infecting the entire target population and guaranteeing a death rate. 100%.
