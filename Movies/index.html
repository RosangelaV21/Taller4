Resident Evil is an action-horror film series based on the Japanese video game franchise of the same name by Capcom. German studio Constantin Film bought the rights to adapt the series in January 1998. In 2001, Screen Gems acquired distribution rights and hired Paul W. S. Anderson as writer and director for Resident Evil (2002). Anderson continued as writer and producer for Resident Evil: Apocalypse (2004) and Resident Evil: Extinction (2007), and returned as the director for Resident Evil: Afterlife (2010), Resident Evil: Retribution (2012), and Resident Evil: The Final Chapter (2016).


The first six films follow Alice (Milla Jovovich), a character created for the films. Alice is a former security specialist and covert operative who battles the Umbrella Corporation, whose bioweapons have triggered a zombie apocalypse. Characters from the games appear, including Claire Redfield, Jill Valentine, Ada Wong, Carlos Olivera, Chris Redfield, Leon S. Kennedy, Barry Burton and antagonists Albert Wesker, the head of Umbrella Corporation, and James Marcus, Umbrella's top virologist.

Characters

Ada Wong

Ada Wong might be the live-action movies' best attempt at adapting a character from the games. In both pieces of media, she has short hair, likes to wear red dresses, and is morally ambiguous.

Leon Kennedy

Leon S. Kennedy is not such a recurring character nor does he play such an important role. He first appeared in Resident Evil: Retribution.

Albert Wesker

Albert Wesker is arguably the best villain the games have to offer. And he serves as the main antagonist for much of the series. The movie version plays a large role also, but he's not quite the main bad guy.

Chris Redfield

During the game series, Chris Redfield features in more entries than anyone else. The character is not as prevalent in the movies, though. He only appears in Afterlife, where he joins a group of survivors led by Alice.

Jill Valentine

Jill Valentine is Resident Evil's ultimate heroine, or at least she is during the games. In the movies, her heroics are usually overshadowed by Alice. Jill still is a major part of the series, especially during her run as a villain in the later installments.

Claire Redfield

Claire Redfield is one of the survivors of the global T-Virus infection and the leader of the caravan that appears in Resident Evil: Extinction . As in video games, she is the younger sister of Chris Redfield .

Alice

Alice , whose real name is Janus Prospero , is a fictional character and the main heroine of the Resident Evil film series who has not appeared in any video game in the series

Nemesis

The Nemesis is among the most iconic monsters in Resident Evil history. And the beast is so important to the plot of Resident Evil 3 and Resident Evil: Apocalypse that he counts as a character.

RESIDENT EVIL

In a clandestine genetic research center, a viral outbreak occurs. To contain the leak, the entire facility is sealed and, in principle, it is believed that all employees die, but in reality they become fierce zombies.


RESIDENT EVIL: APOCALYPSE

Alice is one of the survivors of the biochemical disaster that has wiped out the entire population of Racoon City. Subjected to experiments that have modified her genetics, Alice now possesses more strength, senses and dexterity. These skills will be what you will need to use to escape the dangerous life of Racoon City. Along with other survivors, Alice must fight the forces of the Umbrella Corporation, the zombies that plague the city and the most deadly creature: Nemesis.

RESIDENT EVIL: EXTINCTION

Survivors of the Racoon City catastrophe travel across the Nevada desert hoping to reach Alaska. Alice joins the caravan and her fight with the diabolical Umbrella Corp.


RESIDENT EVIL: AFTERLIFE

In a world invaded by the undead, Alice continues her battle against a corporation, stopping the survivors along the way. Alice, an old friend and colleagues are heading to an alleged safe place in Los Angeles. However, instead of finding a sanctuary, they discover a city controlled by zombies and a trap that is to surprise them.

RESIDENT EVIL: RETRIBUTION

Risking her life while fleeing with the help of an elite commando, Alice will tour the recreations of the cities of Tokyo, New York, Moscow and Washington D.C., created inside that clandestine center of the Umbrella Corporation


RESIDENT EVIL: THE FINAL CHAPTER

In a devastated Washington, Alice makes contact with the Red Queen, who assures that only 4,472 humans remain on Earth and that in less than 48 hours there will be none left. Umbrella’s artificial intelligence asks Alice to stop the apocalypse by locating an aerial antivirus in Raccoon City that can remove the T-virus and everything it has infected.
